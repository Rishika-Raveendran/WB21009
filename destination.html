Kovalam Beach

Over the years the beach became more of an emotion and firmly placed itself on the global tourism map. With its palm-fronted beaches, shimmering sands and a blend of unique cultural and adventurous experiences, it is one of the much-loved beaches in Kerala. This is Kovalam. Life begins with a surreal vibrance at Kovalam. Whether you choose to laze away with your favourite drink and stare into the horizon amid fishing activities, to scuttle through the alleyways experiencing the myriad cultures or get adventurous by indulging in some sporty activities, Kovalam is a melting pot of experiences waiting to be explored by the traveller.Kovalam is formed of three beaches viz. the Hawa Beach or Eve’s beach; Grove’s Beach (Samudra Beach), which is also called Kovalam Beach, and the Light House Beach. The lighthouse beach is famed for its towering lighthouse which offers the best vantage point view of the coastline in southern Kerala.

Ideal time to visit

November to february

Connectivity

Nearest railway station: Thiruvananthapuram Central, about 16 km away.
Nearest airport: Thiruvananthapuram International Airport, about 10 km away

Things to do

Surfing, sunbathing, swimming, snorkeling, catamaran cruise, boating, scuba diving, parasailing, yoga, ayurveda and holistic therapy

Veli Tourist Village

At the Veli Tourist Village, one gets to hop onto a mini-train modelled in the form of a vintage steam engine, experience the steam gushing out of the toy train as one enjoys the joy ride in the scenic surrounds of Veli. As you trundle along the lush surrounds of Veli, feel the wind in your face and meet the child in you. A day full of fun and frolic, that’s what the Veli Tourist Village will do for you. There is never a dull moment here with it being one of the go-to picnic spots in the city. Be it lazing away on the golden sands and watching the sun dip into the horizon or pedaling through the placid waters of the lake, Veli Tourist Village has everything to satiate the wanderlust in you.

Ideal time to visit

All-year destination

Connectivity

Nearest railway station: Thiruvananthapuram Central, about 8 km away.
Nearest airport: Thiruvananthapuram International Airport, about 7 km away

Things to do

Boating, mini-train ride, park and amusement rides

Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple

Located inside the magnificent fort complex in Thiruvananthapuram, the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temple’s history dates back to the 8th century and is one of the 108 sacred Vishnu temples in India. The presiding deity of this temple is Lord Vishnu, reclining on Anantha, the hooded Serpent. About The chamber where the presiding deity is kept is an architectural marvel. Devotees throng the area near the sanctum sanctorum to catch a glimpse of their deity. The idol is about 18 ft long and can be viewed through three different doors. While the head and chest are seen through the first door; the hands can be observed through the second and the feet through the third. The aesthetics and the architecture behind this are often lauded by visitors.

Elephant Rehabilitation Centre, Kappukadu

Life here is jumbo-sized. For the pachyderm inmates at the Elephant Rehabilitation Centre, Kappukadu, a normal day begins with immense pampering, long walks in the woods and indulging in playful fun with each other.A rehabilitation centre modelled as close to the wild as it can be, the facility takes care of baby elephants as well as elephants in their old age. At this one-of-a-kind elephant rehabilitation centre, the focus has always been on creating a space that is in line with the needs of the pachyderms. The centre has open enclosures, and elephants are taken for walks in the reserve forest under the ABP.

Ideal time to visit

All-year destination

Nearest airport: Thiruvananthapuram International Airport, about 40 km away

Things to do

Boating, Trekking, Hiking, Wildlife

Poovar

Sunrise and sunsets at Poovar are quite famous and the best way to experience them is by drifting across the azure blue waters. Plenty of boating options are available for the traveller here and if one needs to try their hands at traditional fishing techniques or other indigenous art and craft making, one needs to just seek the help of a local village person. If you are a beach bum, you can opt to explore the sandy beach which is known for its serene, solitary stretch. To experience the surreal pace of life and its quietude, head to Poovar.

Ideal time to visit

All-year destination

Connectivity

Nearest railway station: Thiruvananthapuram Central, about 23 km away.
Nearest airport: Thiruvananthapuram International Airport, about 25 km away

Things to do

Boating, beach tour, sunbathing, swimming, fishing, village tour and shopping

Shanghumugham Beach

This favourite haunt of locals and travellers alike is noted for the magnificent sculptures that add a special drama to the beach. Matsyakanyaka or Jalakanyaka, a gigantic, 35-m long sculpture of a reclining mermaid which appears to have risen from the sea only to spend some time lazing away on the beach, is an iconic structure that continues to induce a sense of awe and wonderment among visitors. According to its sculptor Kanayi Kunhiraman, the mermaid is a tribute to the sea.The beach is also noted for the Chacha Nehru Traffic Training Park, a training park-cum-recreational centre where children can pedal away and learn the traffic rules as well. The beach is also a spot favoured by the locals to watch the sunset

Ideal time to visit

All-year destination

Connectivity

Nearest airport: Thiruvananthapuram International Airport is a short drive away

Things to do

Sunbathing, swimming, beach tour, amusement rides, culture trail, spiritual tour and children’s park

Neyyar Dam and Park

Here, you experience the wilderness like no other. The Neyyar Dam is a picturesque picnic spot that is frequented by locals and travellers alike. It is part of the Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary, a verdant oasis, which boasts of a lush 12,000 hectares of natural vegetation. The rich flora and fauna of the sanctuary along with the many trekking trails is any nature enthusiast’s dream come true. The Watch Tower, Deer Park, Lion Safari Park and Crocodile Rehabilitation and Research Centre at Neyyar offer a wholesome travel experience replete with fun and blissful moments. At the reservoir, one may avail of the boating facilities where you can sail through the gorgeous waters of Neyyar lake and experience the panoramic view of the area

Nearest airport: Thiruvananthapuram International Airport, about 38 km away

Things to do

Trekking, hiking, nature, camping, bird watching, boating, wildlife and adventure sports

Ponmudi

Be it the constant cackle of the birds or the occasional sighting of the wild beings, Ponmudi has everything in store for the nature enthusiast in you. Located at about 915 ft above sea level and home to a diverse species of flora and fauna, Ponmudi is a paradise for trekkers. The glorious Meenmutty waterfalls and the gorgeous Kallar river add to the attraction. Plenty of trekking options in the woods are available in the hill station. One of the most famous trails is the trekking trail to Varayadumotta, an 18km-long trek, which offers a panoramic view of the hills along with the Nilgiri Tahrs.

Ideal time to visit

November to March

Connectivity

Nearest railway station: Thiruvananthapuram Central, about 61 km away.
Nearest airport: Thiruvananthapuram International Airport, about 58 km away

Things to do

Trekking, Hiking, Cycling, Nature, Camping

Kuthiramalika

The palace showcases intricate carvings and magnificent pillars. The horses are carved into the wooden wall brackets of the palace and make for a resplendent sight.Architectural enthusiasts, painters and history buffs will have plenty to explore here, starting with the architecture and craftsmanship. Every year the palace hosts a special musical extravaganza titled the Swathi Sangeetholsavam or Swathi Music Festival where erstwhile king Swathi Thirunal Rama Varma’s compositions are rendered. This famed festival is attended by music connoisseurs and travellers alike and is much revered in the music fraternity.

Ideal time to visit

All-year destination

Connectivity

Nearest railway station: Thiruvananthapuram Central, about 1 km away.
Nearest airport: Thiruvananthapuram International Airport, about 6 km away

Things to do

Museum tour, culture & heritage trail, spiritual tour and shopping

Akkulam

Resting on the banks of Akkulam Lake, the tourist village has attractions suiting people of all age — boat club, musical fountain, artificial waterfall, bicycle track, swimming pool, children’s park and cafeteria. Amid a calm and serene atmosphere, all the members of a family will find something that suits their interest at this backwater destination. Located around 10km from the city, Akkulam provides an excellent view of calm backwaters, swaying coconut groves and thick foliage. Offering a bouquet of rides, the Boat Club is the primary attraction in Akkulam. The ride between Akkulam and Veli amid beautiful and wide backwaters will provide a thrilling experience to visitors

Connectivity

Nearest railway station: Thiruvananthapuram Central, about 10 km away.
Nearest airport: Thiruvananthapuram International Airport, about 7 km away

Things to do

Boating, Cycling, Swimming
